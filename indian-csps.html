Common Questions

Answers on Indian CSPs & Sovereign Cloud

Questions we hear from CIOs, compliance heads, and procurement teams every week.

What makes an Indian CSP genuinely "sovereign" compared to AWS Mumbai? ▾

AWS Mumbai is a data centre physically in India but owned by a US corporation. Under the US CLOUD Act, US law enforcement can compel AWS to produce data regardless of where it is stored. An Indian CSP like ESDS, Yotta, or CtrlS is an Indian legal entity — Indian law is the only jurisdiction that applies. This distinction is critical for government workloads, defence contractors, and BFSI entities where legal sovereignty matters just as much as physical location.

Which government entities can procure Indian CSPs through Thynkwise? ▾

Any central ministry, state government department, PSU, autonomous body, smart city SPV, or local body subject to GFR 2017 procurement rules can procure Indian CSP services through Thynkwise. All our Indian CSP partners are MeITY empanelled — the mandatory qualification for government cloud procurement. Thynkwise provides GFR-compliant quotations, GeM integration support, and full managed services post-deployment.

How do I choose between ESDS, Yotta, CtrlS, Sify, E2E, and NTT? ▾

Each Indian CSP has a distinct strength: ESDS for regulated banking (BFSI Community Cloud, 280+ banks); Yotta for hyperscale compute at scale (NM1 campus); CtrlS for Tier IV certified facilities across 7 cities; Sify for hybrid cloud and enterprise SD-WAN; E2E Networks for AI/ML GPU workloads at 60–70% below hyperscaler pricing; NTT for global enterprise and MNC requirements. Most enterprises use 2–3 Indian CSPs alongside one hyperscaler for a hybrid architecture. Thynkwise's free workload assessment maps your portfolio to the right combination and manages all of them.

Is the ESDS BFSI Community Cloud suitable for cooperative banks? ▾

Yes — the BFSI Community Cloud is specifically designed for cooperative banks, RRBs, and DCCBs. The shared-cost model makes enterprise-grade RBI-compliant infrastructure accessible to smaller banking entities who cannot justify a dedicated cloud environment. Core banking hosting, disaster recovery, and statutory audit infrastructure are all pre-configured. 280+ banks currently run on this platform. Thynkwise handles procurement, migration, and ongoing managed operations.

Can private companies use Indian CSPs, or is this only for government and banks? ▾

Indian CSPs serve both public sector and private organisations. Any company with strict data sovereignty requirements — IRDAI-regulated insurers, NBFC-MFIs, healthcare entities under DISHA, or enterprises with board-level policies on data residency — benefits from Indian CSPs. E2E Networks is particularly popular with AI/ML startups and enterprises that need GPU compute at Indian cost levels. Thynkwise also designs multi-cloud architectures where sovereign-sensitive workloads sit on Indian CSPs while other applications run on AWS or Azure.

Yes. When you engage Thynkwise as your managed Indian CSP partner, you receive a single Thynkwise SLA that covers your entire Indian CSP environment regardless of which partners are deployed. Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know — answered clearly.

What is Indian sovereign cloud?

Indian sovereign cloud refers to cloud infrastructure hosted entirely within India, operated by Indian companies — ensuring data residency, compliance with Indian regulations (RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, DPDP Act), and sovereignty over data without dependency on foreign hyperscalers.

Which Indian cloud providers does Thynkwise support?

Thynkwise manages services across ESDS (eNlight Cloud), Yotta Infrastructure, CtrlS Datacenters (Tier IV), Sify Technologies, E2E Networks, and NTT India — all major Indian sovereign cloud and colocation providers.

When should I use Indian sovereign cloud vs AWS or Azure?

Indian sovereign cloud is preferred for workloads requiring strict data residency within India, regulatory mandates (RBI NBFC data localisation, government data), predictable INR billing, and proximity-sensitive applications. AWS/Azure are preferred for global scale, advanced AI/ML services, and enterprise SaaS integrations.

What is the DPDP Act and how does it affect cloud choice?

India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act mandates how personal data of Indian citizens must be processed and stored. Indian sovereign cloud providers offer compliant data residency guarantees that simplify DPDP adherence — particularly for BFSI, healthcare, and consumer internet businesses.
